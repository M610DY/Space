Black Hole


A black hole is an area of spacetime with such tremendous gravity that nothing, not even light or other electromagnetic waves, can escape its event horizon. According to general relativity theory, a sufficiently compact mass may bend spacetime to generate a black hole.

The Milky Way


The Milky Way is the galaxy that contains the Solar System, and the term refers to how the galaxy appears from Earth: a hazy strip of light seen in the night sky generated by stars that cannot be identified individually with the human eye.

Our Solar System


The Solar System is composed of the Sun and the objects that orbit it. It was created by the gravitational collapse of a massive interstellar molecular cloud 4.6 billion years ago. The Sun contains the great bulk of the system's mass, with Jupiter holding the majority of the remaining mass.
